Company Overview
What They Are
Public Health Management Corporation (PHMC) is a Philadelphia-based nonprofit public health institute founded in 1973. PHMC creates and sustains healthier communities through direct service, partnerships, innovation, policy research, technical assistance, and workforce development. They operate 200+ programs across 70+ locations in the Delaware Valley (Philadelphia + Delaware), serving approximately 350,000 clients annually.
Organizational Structure
PHMC is the parent organization over a network of subsidiaries and programs:
• PHMC Health — FQHCs (Federally Qualified Health Centers)
• Turning Points for Children (TPFC) — child welfare programs
• Joseph J. Peters Institute — behavioral health
• Health Promotion Council — health advocacy; won $100K Tobacco Award 2026
• Center for Autism — autism services
• PHMC Public Health Campus on Cedar — community health hub
• Pathways to Recovery — substance use recovery (expanding 2026)
• Community Behavioral Health (CVCA) — behavioral health subsidiary
